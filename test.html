Sorry, My Bestie

“In the quiet spaces between words,
I send my heart — a humble apology,
Wrapped in vintage hues and tender poetry.
Please forgive me, dear friend.”

Words From the Heart

In the quiet dawn of our friendship, shadows fell between us, Like silent whispers carried by a gentle breeze, I stand here with a heart laid bare, hoping these words Can bridge the distance, heal the fractures unseen. Forgive me, dear bestie, for the times I faltered, For the words left unspoken and moments misplaced. Our bond is a tapestry woven with laughter and tears, And though frayed, it holds the strength of countless memories. Let my apology be the light that guides us back, Where understanding blooms and trust is renewed. For friendship, like the rarest flower, deserves care, And my soul longs to nurture what was almost lost. So hear me now, with an open heart and gentle grace— I’m sorry, my best friend, for every hurt and every silence, And I vow to cherish you more, come what may.

“Sorry is the soul’s poetry.”
“Forgive me, my bestie.”
“From my heart, always.”
“Mending bridges with words.”
